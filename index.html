Heres Linus Torvalds time line

  • 1969: Linus Torvalds was born on December 28 in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1981: At age 10, Torvalds began to dabble in computer programming on his grandfather’s Commodore VIC-20.
  • 1991: Linus Torvalds created the Linux Operating System1. On August 24, he announced Linux on the comp.os.minix Usenet newsgroup3. On September 17, he released Linux kernel v0.01, the first-ever Linux release.
  • 1992: On February 1, Linux officially became open source with a GNU software license.
  • 1999: Linus was a recipient of the EFF Pioneer Award.
  • 2000: He was given the title of ‘one of the top inventors, under the age of 35, in the world’ by MIT Technology Review TR100.
  • 2001: He was the recipient of the Lovelace Medal, which was conferred by the British Computer Society.
  • 2005: Linus Torvalds is also the original author of Git, another open-source software, this time for distributed version control.
  • 2012: His achievements have been recognized with many international awards in his field, including the induction into the Internet Society’s Global INET Internet Hall of Fame.
  • 2014: He received the IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award.
  • 2018: He received the IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award.

His creation of Linux transformed computing and software development through its open-source, collaborative approach and has become one of the largest free software projects in the world. Linus Torvalds has made significant contributions to computing through the creation of the Linux kernel and, later, the development of the Git control system.
